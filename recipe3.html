Puerto Rican Pasteles Recipe

History:

Puerto Rican pasteles have a rich history influenced by Taíno, African, and Spanish cultures. These savory treats, made with a dough of green plantains and yuca, are traditionally filled with seasoned meat. The process of making pasteles is often a communal event, especially during the holidays, symbolizing family and cultural heritage. Pasteles are more than just food; they are a celebration of Puerto Rican identity.

Description

Pasteles are similar to tamales, featuring a flavorful filling wrapped in a dough made from grated plantains and yuca. These delicious bundles are typically steamed and served during festive occasions, embodying the warmth of Puerto Rican hospitality.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups green plantains (peeled and grated)
  • 2 cups yuca (peeled and grated)
  • 1 lb pork (shredded)
  • 1/2 cup olives (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup capers
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the filling by sautéing onions and garlic, then add pork, olives, capers, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook until flavors meld.
  2. In a large bowl, mix grated plantains and yuca to form a dough-like consistency.
  3. Lay out banana leaves, add a scoop of the dough, and place filling on top. Fold and wrap securely.
  4. Steam pasteles for about 1-2 hours until firm.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
